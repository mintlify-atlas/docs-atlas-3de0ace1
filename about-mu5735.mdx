China Eastern Airlines Flight MU5735 was a scheduled passenger flight operating from Kunming Changshui International Airport to Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport. On March 21, 2022, while cruising at approximately 29,000 feet, the aircraft entered a sudden and rapid descent and impacted terrain near Wuzhou, China. All 132 people on board were killed. The accident is investigated by the Civil Aviation Administration of China (CAAC) with the NTSB participating as the state-of-manufacture representative.

## FDR data termination

Both engine fuel cutoff switches were moved to the **cutoff** position while the aircraft was cruising at approximately 29,000 feet. This caused both engines to lose power, and both engines' N2 (high-pressure spool speed) values dropped below the threshold required to sustain the integrated drive generators. Once the generators disconnected, the FDR lost electrical power and recording stopped at approximately **26,000 feet** during the descent.

The FDR storage module was severely damaged on impact. The NTSB performed detailed physical repair and data recovery work — including connector pin restoration, modified recovery cables, and direct FLASH chip reads — to extract usable data from the damaged hardware.

<Note>
  Unlike the FDR, the HFR5-V CVR has an internal battery backup. The CVR continued to record cockpit audio for more than 10 minutes after the FDR stopped, capturing audio through impact. Both storage modules sustained severe impact damage. After extensive physical repair — including connector housing removal and pin rebuilding — all four CVR channels were ultimately recovered at "Excellent" quality.
</Note>
