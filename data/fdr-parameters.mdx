---
title: "Validated FDR Parameters: Boeing 737-800 Data Frame"
description: "All 150+ validated flight data recorder parameters from the B737-800 data frame, organized by system category with units and scale factor notes."
---

The Boeing 737-800 flight data recorder (FDR) aboard China Eastern flight MU5735 (NTSB case DCA22WA102, aircraft B-1791) captured over 150 discrete parameters spanning electrical buses, inertial sensors, flight controls, engines, autopilot modes, and cockpit inputs. NTSB analysts validated each parameter against the aircraft's data frame layout using CIDER (version Scratch 007-ParameterValidation), verifying scale factors and biases to produce the two derived datasets — `ExactSample.csv` and `TableResolution.csv` — released under FOIA.

The sections below list every validated parameter by category, with its engineering unit where applicable.

<Note>
  All parameters were validated against the Boeing 737-800 airframe data frame definition. Scale factors and biases were individually checked before the data was exported from CIDER. Discrete (enumerated) parameters such as switch positions are encoded as numeric values in the CSV files; the raw encoding key (e.g., `0.0 = RUN`, `1.0 = CUTOFF`) is preserved in the header rows of both CSV files.
</Note>

<Note>
  FLASH chip U2 on the FDR storage board was cracked through its silicon die and could not be read. This causes periodic data gaps of approximately 1.3 seconds in the raw FDR recording. NTSB analysts manually corrected the affected portion covering the final 12 minutes of recorded flight; the corrected data is what appears in `ExactSample.csv` and `TableResolution.csv`.
</Note>
