The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), 5 U.S.C. § 552, gives the public the right to request records from U.S. federal agencies, including the National Transportation Safety Board. When the NTSB participates in a foreign accident investigation — as it did for MU5735 (case **DCA22WA102**) — it produces technical reports, recorder download analyses, and internal correspondence that are subject to FOIA requests once the investigation progresses. You can submit a FOIA request directly to the NTSB to obtain documents related to any investigation in which the agency has participated.

## NTSB documents and the public domain

Records produced by officers or employees of the U.S. government as part of their official duties are works of the federal government and are **not subject to copyright protection** under 17 U.S.C. § 105. The NTSB recorder report and associated data files in this archive are works of the U.S. government and are in the **public domain**. You may reproduce, distribute, and build upon them without restriction.

## How this archive was assembled

The materials in this repository were originally obtained through FOIA requests and shared publicly by another GitHub user. That repository was subsequently deleted or made private by its owner. This archive re-hosts the same files — without modifying them and without preserving the original commit history — so that the public record remains accessible. The original contributor's identity is not disclosed here in order to respect their privacy.

## What the FOIA materials cover

The documents in this archive relate specifically to the NTSB's technical participation in the MU5735 investigation. They include:

- The **combined CVR and FDR download report** (dated July 1, 2022), which describes recorder recovery methods, data quality, and the extracted flight data parameters
- **FOIA response letters** from the NTSB documenting the release of these records
- **Supplementary data files** containing the validated FDR parameter set and time-series flight data from the final 12 minutes of the flight

These materials do not constitute the official final accident report, which is produced by the CAAC as the investigating state. They represent the NTSB's technical contribution as the accredited representative of the state of manufacture.
